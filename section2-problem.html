The Problem

Your dog has been trying
to tell you something.
You just couldn't hear it.

Dogs can't speak. And by the time most health issues become visible — the limping, the lethargy, the loss of appetite — they've often been building for days. The tools we rely on weren't built to close that gap.

📷
Your camera sees everything. It tells you nothing.

Home cameras show you what's happening but can't interpret it. A dog lying down all day looks the same whether he's resting or in pain.

📍
GPS tracks where your dog goes. Not how your dog feels.

Every major smart collar on the market is built around location. That's useful if your dog escapes. It tells you nothing about their health.

🏥
Your vet sees your dog once a year. For twenty minutes.

A snapshot exam is the only data your vet has to work with. They're making decisions without ever seeing what your dog's normal actually looks like.

⏰
Eight hours alone at home. Anything could happen.

Most dog owners are away from home all day. If something goes wrong at 9am, you may not know until 6pm. That window is too long.

Days
Before owners notice

Health problems in dogs don't appear overnight — they build quietly over days.

Changes in eating, drinking, sleep, breathing, and behavior are the earliest warning signs. Without continuous monitoring, those signals go unnoticed until the problem is serious — and the vet bill is bigger.
